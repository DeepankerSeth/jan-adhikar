JAN ADHIKAR — EVIDENCE PRESERVATION CHECKLIST
Last reviewed: 22 July 2026

IMMEDIATE STEPS
[ ] Put safety and medical care first.
[ ] Keep the original photo, video, audio or document untouched.
[ ] Do not crop, filter, annotate, compress, rename or repeatedly export the original.
[ ] Create a separate working copy for viewing, redaction or public sharing.
[ ] Back up the original in at least two secure places controlled by trusted people.
[ ] Record the original filename, file size, format and device used.
[ ] Record who created it, exact date/time, location and what it depicts.
[ ] Record whether the creator personally witnessed the event.
[ ] Obtain the creator's permission before giving the file to others.
[ ] Record every transfer: from whom, to whom, date/time, method and purpose.

FOR PHOTOS, VIDEO AND AUDIO
[ ] Preserve embedded metadata where possible.
[ ] Write a short description without guessing identities or motives.
[ ] Record visible landmarks, signs, uniforms, vehicles and sequence of events.
[ ] Preserve footage immediately before and after the key moment.
[ ] Note whether the device clock was accurate.
[ ] Do not publish unblurred faces of vulnerable people without consent.

FOR INJURY OR DETENTION
[ ] Obtain medical records, prescriptions, bills and medico-legal documents.
[ ] Photograph injuries over time with consent and a date reference.
[ ] Preserve arrest memo, FIR/DD number, bail papers and court orders.
[ ] Record police station, officer/unit, time of custody and time of release.
[ ] Record requests for a lawyer, family contact and medical care, and responses.

SECURE STORAGE
[ ] Restrict access to named people.
[ ] Use strong passwords and device encryption.
[ ] Do not store Aadhaar, medical records, home addresses or detainee data in a public drive.
[ ] Keep an access log.
[ ] If technically able, calculate a SHA-256 hash and record it in the chain-of-custody log.

PUBLIC SHARING
[ ] Share only a redacted copy.
[ ] Remove addresses, phone numbers, identity numbers and medical details.
[ ] Blur faces where publication could create danger, stigma or retaliation.
[ ] Label uncertain details as unverified.
[ ] Do not accuse a named individual unless identification and facts are supportable.
[ ] Correct public errors promptly while preserving the original record.

LEGAL HANDOVER
[ ] Give counsel an indexed copy, not a loose set of links.
[ ] Retain the untouched original.
[ ] Identify the person who can verify each item by affidavit or testimony.
[ ] Ask counsel whether a formal preservation notice should be sent.

This checklist is general legal information, not legal advice. Do not delay emergency, medical or legal assistance to complete documentation.
