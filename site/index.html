What this tool can and cannot do

It helps you organize facts and reach official channels. It does not file a court case, prove an allegation, replace a lawyer, or guarantee action by an authority.

Preserve evidence

A reliable record is more useful than a viral post.

Keep originals untouched. Record who created each file, when, where, and how it moved between people. Share redacted copies publicly and keep originals private for counsel or investigators.

Know the legal tools

Different problems require different legal routes.

Article 14

Equality and non-arbitrariness

Government action must not be arbitrary or unlawfully discriminatory.

Article 19

Speech and peaceful assembly

Citizens have protected freedoms, subject to constitutionally permitted reasonable restrictions.

Article 21

Life and personal liberty

State action affecting bodily integrity, dignity or liberty must follow fair, just and lawful procedure.

Article 22

Safeguards after arrest

Subject to constitutional exceptions, an arrested person must be told the grounds, may consult a lawyer, and must be produced before the nearest magistrate within 24 hours, excluding travel time.

Article 32

Supreme Court writ jurisdiction

A formal writ petition asks the Supreme Court to enforce fundamental rights. An email or letter may request PIL screening but does not itself open a case.

Article 226

High Court writ jurisdiction

A High Court can issue writs for fundamental rights and other legal rights. Local incidents may be better suited to the relevant High Court.
