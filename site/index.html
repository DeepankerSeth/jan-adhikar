Detained or missing

First decide: suspected police custody, or genuinely missing?

Known or suspected police custody

Call legal aid and the likely police station or district control room. Ask for the BNSS section 37 designated officer and arrest information.

Use Delhi Police's missing-person helpline. Do not use 1094 merely to confirm a suspected arrest.

Immediate danger: 112

Write down these facts

  • Full name and safe contact number
  • Last confirmed time and exact location
  • Police station, unit or vehicle, if known
  • FIR, DD, arrest memo or custody reference, if available
  • Every station called: time, person spoken to and response
Essential safeguards after arrest

Subject to constitutional exceptions, the arrested person must be told the grounds, has the right to consult and be defended by a lawyer of choice, and must be produced before the nearest magistrate within 24 hours excluding journey time. Ask for the arrest memo; immediate notice to the nominated person of both arrest and place of custody; a medical examination that records injuries and a report copy; and an opportunity to meet an advocate during interrogation, though not throughout it.

Say this:

“I need urgent legal help for [name]. They were [injured/detained/missing] at [place] around [time]. The last confirmed information is [fact]. Please tell me the next immediate legal step.”

A person in custody qualifies for legal services under section 12(g). In other cases, lawyer assignment depends on eligibility, case assessment and availability. Email is not an emergency channel.

Serious Delhi Police misconduct

For death or grievous hurt in custody, rape or attempted rape in custody, arrest or detention without due process, extortion, land or house grabbing, or serious abuse of authority. Complaints by a victim or representative should be signed and supported by a sworn affidavit; anonymous complaints are excluded. PCA may not entertain a matter already before a court, NHRC or another statutory body.

Email is only an intake method—not proof of registration. Ask for acknowledgment and call to confirm. Postal: Police Complaints Authority, 10th Floor, Chanderlok Building, Janpath, New Delhi 110001.

Official PCA contact page · pca.delhi@delhi.gov.in · 011-21400977
Human-rights complaint involving a public servant

For a violation or abetment by a public servant, or public-servant negligence in preventing one. NHRC is not emergency dispatch. It ordinarily screens out matters older than one year, sub judice, vague or anonymous complaints and service matters. Its actions are principally inquiries, recommendations, interim-relief recommendations or court approaches—not guaranteed compensation or prosecution.

Open NHRC complaint portal · 14433
Court action or release from unlawful custody
